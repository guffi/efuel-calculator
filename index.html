Electricity Price (€/MWh)
?
Cost per MWh of input electricity. For solar-powered projects, use your LCOE. A lower electricity price is the single biggest lever in eFuel economics. Also drives Brineworks TEA-optimized parameters.
Capacity Factor (%)
?
The fraction of time the electrolyzer runs at full capacity. Solar PV: 15–25%. Wind: 30–45%. Grid-connected: up to 90%. Higher capacity factors spread CAPEX across more operating hours, reducing capital cost per unit of output.
Discount Rate / WACC (%)
?
Your cost of capital (WACC). This determines how heavily upfront CAPEX is weighted vs. ongoing OPEX. A 6% rate might apply for project finance; 10–12% for higher-risk early-stage projects.
Project Lifetime (years)
?
Economic lifetime over which CAPEX is amortised. Used with WACC to compute the Capital Recovery Factor (CRF = WACC×(1+WACC)ⁿ / ((1+WACC)ⁿ−1)), which determines the annual capital charge per tonne. Longer lifetimes reduce cost per tonne.
